## About Me

I am a remote sensing and GIS researcher specializing in vegetation dynamics and ecological monitoring. My work integrates satellite imagery (Landsat, Sentinel-2, MODIS), UAV data, and field observations with machine learning and ecological modeling to understand ecosystem change across space and time.

My earlier research focused on peatland vegetation in boreal and subarctic regions, contributing to the understanding of plant phenology and spatial patterns. Currently, I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Smithsonian National Zoo & Conservation Biology Institute, where I apply these approaches to bustard conservation by linking vegetation dynamics with species habitat modeling to support data-driven conservation strategies.
